Pure white and cloud-like, white cake can be both a sophisticated, layered wedding cake or a perfect blank canvas for a funfetti cake. Unlike yellow cake, white cake typically uses all egg whites, no yolks. This means less fat and more sponginess. White cake recipes might also call for a combination of butter and shortening to tone down the yellow hue of a pure butter cake. White cake traditionally calls for cake flour rather than all-purpose, which makes for a thinner batter and a lighter cake. Cake flour is bleached for a pure white color and has a lower protein content, which reduces the amount of gluten that forms inside.
